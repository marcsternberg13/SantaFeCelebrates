The Concept

The City Becomes the Canvas

Immersive at Architectural Scale

Santa Fe Celebrates is a month-long public art event that transforms Santa Fe's most iconic landmarks — the Plaza, the Cathedral Basilica of St. Francis of Assisi, Loretto Chapel, La Fonda on the Plaza, and city parks — into monumental living canvases.

The work of Cherokee-Choctaw master painter Poteet Victory is projected at architectural scale across facades and public spaces each night in August 2026. Palette-knife built, abstract, spiritually charged paintings that have never been seen at this scale. The city itself becomes the frame.

The immersive technology is led by Massimiliano ("Max") Siccardi — the world's most-produced creator of large-scale immersive experiences and creative director of the globally acclaimed Van Gogh Immersive, seen by millions across 30+ cities on five continents.

A New Annual Tradition

Santa Fe Celebrates is designed to recur each August — rotating featured artist, deepening the city's identity as the world capital of Indigenous art.

Year One anchors the event to the Victory on the Big Screen media slate: the Victory narrative feature film, the Outside the Frame docuseries, and The Invisible Canvas immersive experience — all centered on Poteet Victory.

By owning August, Santa Fe claims a month that already draws 150,000 visitors for Indian Market — the largest Indigenous art market in the world — and extends the cultural moment into an event that fills hotels for a month, not a weekend.

Year One · Featured Artist

Poteet
Victory

Cherokee-Choctaw · Idabel, Oklahoma · Santa Fe

His name comes from two worlds that shaped everything he ever made. Poteet, from his mother's Cajun side. Victory, from his grandmother Willie Victory — a full-blood Cherokee-Choctaw woman whose storytelling planted the seed of every painting that followed.

Born in Idabel, Oklahoma in 1947, 90 miles from the end of the Trail of Tears. Bull rider at thirteen. Hippie commune in Maui. A silk-screen empire in Dallas with clients including Willie Nelson, Waylon Jennings, CBS Records, and Frito-Lay. Then he sold it, met Andy Warhol, studied at the Art Students League, moved to Santa Fe in 1989 with nothing — and invented an entirely new visual language.

He abandoned the brush entirely in favor of the palette knife, building paintings in dense, glossy layers that carry Indigenous symbols and spiritual archetypes without ever illustrating them literally. Gallery owners say the work sells before it dries.

He is now the first inductee to the Semple Museum of Native American Art Hall of Fame, a Smithsonian collection artist, owner of Victory Contemporary Gallery at 124 W. Palace Ave., Santa Fe — and the subject of a full biography by J. Robert Keating (Atmosphere Press, 2022).

What Immersive
Public Art Does to a City

Three cities have already proven what happens when you transform landmark architecture into public art. The data isn't aspirational — it's documented. Cincinnati built $126M in economic impact from a 4-day event. Amsterdam fills hotels for seven weeks. Barcelona draws 300,000 people in 3 nights. Santa Fe Celebrates deploys the same proven mechanism — anchored to 150,000 visitors already coming for Indian Market. The infrastructure exists. The audience exists. The art exists.

Financial & Non-Financial Impact

Economic Return

The 2022 edition generated a $126M direct economic impact for the Cincinnati region across hotels, food, drink, retail, and travel — from a 4-day free event. The 2024 edition grew that to $142M in total spending, with 15 countries represented in attendance.

Artist Economy

$1.5M in direct artist commissions, honorariums, and art fees in 2022 alone — establishing Blink as an engine for the creative sector, not just a tourism driver. 71 artists from 18 countries plus local talent.

City Identity

Cincinnati Mayor Aftab Pureval: "BLINK has firmly established itself as a cultural icon that reverberates energy and excitement across the globe." The event repositioned Cincinnati as a world-class art destination — a city people travel to specifically for the experience.

Community Cohesion

Blink is free and covers 30 city blocks — including historically underinvested neighborhoods. 2 million people of every demographic sharing the same public space for the same experience. The social capital generated by that shared moment is immeasurable and irreplaceable.

A city with no pre-existing claim to global art status built $126–142M in economic impact and a lasting international reputation — from 4 days and public architecture it already owned. Cincinnati now goes international, announcing expansion beyond North America for 2026.

Financial & Non-Financial Impact

Tourism Extension

The Amsterdam Light Festival was designed for one explicit purpose: extend the tourism season into winter, a traditionally low-traffic period. It now draws over a million visitors annually — consistently filling hotels during what had been the city's quietest months.

Hotel Economy

Amsterdam now faces the opposite of a tourism problem — the city has imposed a cap of 20 million overnight stays annually and banned new hotel construction. The Light Festival helped build a year-round demand profile so strong the city has to limit it.

Cultural Positioning

The festival transforms Amsterdam's UNESCO-listed canals into an open-air gallery — reinforcing the city's identity as a UNESCO Creative City of Design. Fourteen editions in, it is a defining feature of Amsterdam's global cultural brand.

The Annual Flywheel

Seven consecutive weeks of nightly programming means seven consecutive weeks of hotel occupancy, restaurant revenue, retail spending. A single weekend event generates a spike; a month-long event generates a sustained economic ecosystem. This is the Amsterdam model — and it is the Santa Fe Celebrates model.

Amsterdam built a 7-week tourism anchor in its slowest season, now drawing 1M+ visitors annually. The cultural identity reinforcement was so successful the city's tourism problem flipped from too little to too much. That's the power of owning a season.

Financial & Non-Financial Impact

Tourism in Slow Season

Llum BCN takes place in February — the quietest month of the year for Barcelona tourism. It draws 300,000 visitors in 3 nights, generating substantial revenue for hotels, restaurants, and retail in a period that would otherwise underperform. It was designed as a stimulus, and it works.

Neighborhood Transformation

The festival is anchored in Poblenou, once an industrial district. Llum BCN positioned Poblenou as Barcelona's creative and innovation hub — attracting tech companies, design studios, and cultural institutions in its wake. Light art as urban economic development.

Municipal Investment Logic

Llum BCN is organized and funded by the Barcelona City Council's Institut de Cultura. The city treats it as infrastructure — a recurring investment that pays returns in tourism, identity, and civic pride. The $2M ask for Santa Fe Celebrates follows this exact model.

International Recognition

Barcelona is the 2026 World Capital of Architecture — and Llum BCN is woven into that identity. The festival proves that projection mapping on existing architecture is not a spectacle separate from cultural identity; it is cultural identity, expressed at scale.

Barcelona fills its slowest 3 nights with 300,000 visitors — while simultaneously repositioning a neighborhood and reinforcing a global cultural brand. The mechanism is identical to what Santa Fe Celebrates deploys. The only difference is scale: Santa Fe starts with an audience of 150,000 already present for Indian Market.

Why Santa Fe Leads the Way

The Unfair Advantage

Cincinnati had to build its audience from scratch. Amsterdam had to create a winter season. Barcelona had to fill a slow month. Santa Fe already has 150,000 people arriving in August for Indian Market — the largest Indigenous art market in the world. The audience is already there. Santa Fe Celebrates gives them a reason to stay longer and come back.

The Art Already Exists

Poteet Victory has spent 35 years building one of the most significant bodies of work in contemporary Indigenous painting. The paintings exist. The artist is here. The gallery is here. No other city has access to this material. Santa Fe Celebrates is not importing culture — it is amplifying what is already the city's most powerful asset.

Extending the Season

Indian Market is one weekend. Santa Fe Celebrates is a month. That means 31 nights of hotel occupancy where there was previously a spike and a drop. Restaurants, galleries, retail — all sustained at Indian Market levels for four weeks instead of two days. The economic multiplier is substantial and immediate.

An Annual Platform

Year One is Poteet Victory. Year Two is a new Indigenous master artist. Year Three, another. Over a decade, Santa Fe Celebrates becomes the defining cultural event in the American Southwest — building an identity as the world capital of Indigenous art, not just the world's largest Indigenous art market.

Cincinnati built $142M in impact with a 4-day event and no pre-existing art tourism infrastructure. Santa Fe starts with $200M in Indian Market impact, 150,000 visitors already present, the 3rd-largest art market in the United States, and the world's most compelling living Indigenous artist. The question is not whether this works. The question is whether Santa Fe leads, or lets another city show it how.

Creative & Producing Leadership

Creative Director · Immersive
Massimiliano
"Max" Siccardi
Creator: Van Gogh · Klimt · Mozart · Frida · Rome & the Vatican · Da Vinci Code (in development)

Massimiliano Siccardi is the world's most-produced creator of large-scale immersive art experiences. His Van Gogh Immersive has been seen by millions across 30+ cities on five continents — described as "the Steven Spielberg of installation art shows."

He curated 60,600 frames of video, 90,000,000 pixels, and 500,000 cubic feet of projections set to original music for the Van Gogh experience alone. Each of his productions begins with the same conviction: the art should be impossible to stand outside of.

Santa Fe Celebrates brings this proven global format to the art of Poteet Victory and the public spaces of Santa Fe. For the first time, a Siccardi-scale production will be applied to a living Indigenous artist's work — outdoors, across an entire city, for a month.
